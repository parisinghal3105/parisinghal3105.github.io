Unity

English Lit• April 2026 • Academic Report

Abstract

In today’s world, news about war and conflict is constantly shared across social media and other platforms, making it difficult to ignore the challenges many countries face. Seeing these events occur because of discrimination has made me reflect on how important peace and unity are for the future of humanity. This report (originally a speech) was written to explore the meaning of world peace and the role that understanding, and mutual respect between anyone and everyone can play in creating a more united world.

Do you feel like you belong?

In a world filled with people and opportunities, why are we so alone? Our world is plagued. Plagued by hatred and views of isolation and separation. As a community, we are divided. Divided by thoughts of self doubt and self hatred that we project onto others. The sense of belonging has slowly vanished because of your hateful acts. Social Media trends promote careless behaviours and isolation are spread across, normalising hate and discrimination. Our world is broken. Today, I aim to inspire you to think differently. I urge you to begin to feel compassion for one another. I want a united world. For us and for our future.

The people are suffering. Suffering from cruel words, discrimination and constant judgement. Your crude comments separate our community. In a world supposedly “more connected than ever”, how are we so divided? Offensive remarks, in all their forms, cause genuine, lasting damage. Studies consistently show that more than half of the teenage population has experienced some form of online harassment. Some have also stated how it has negatively affected their mental health. Words may be invisible, however their longterm impact is painfully real. A world filled with hate and violence cannot be a world of unity. If we allow harmful actions to continue without consequence, we will have accepted division and segregation. This is an unfortunate future that none of us should want.

Unity begins with us taking responsibility. Education plays a crucial role. As a society, we should normalise teaching values of respect and mutual understanding. Research shows that communities that promote respectful dialogue face lower levels of harassment and segregation. As individuals, we must also challenge hate when we see it. Silence allows cruelty to grow. Speaking out creates accountability. Peace is not created through control, but through understanding and mutual respect.

In conclusion, uniting the world doesn’t mean that we should ignore conflict. It means choosing to respond differently. Through empathy, education and collective action, we can rebuild our community into something stronger. Change starts small. The people are suffering – but they do not have to continue. The future depends on the choices we make today. A peaceful world is within reach. For us. For our future.
